# Philosophical Ontology

This document establishes a comprehensive ontological framework for philosophical concepts within the cognitive modeling knowledge base, providing structured relationships between philosophical domains and their foundational role in cognitive science, active inference, and scientific methodology.

## ðŸ§  Core Philosophical Domains

### Metaphysics
The study of the fundamental nature of reality and existence.

#### Ontology (`metaphysical_ontology/`)
- **Being and Existence**: What exists and why it exists
- **Substance and Properties**: Fundamental constituents of reality
- **Universals and Particulars**: Abstract vs concrete entities
- **Causality and Determinism**: How and why things happen
- **Time and Space**: Nature of temporal and spatial reality
- **Mind and Matter**: Relationship between mental and physical

#### Philosophy of Mind (`philosophy_of_mind/`)
- **Dualism**: Mind and body as separate substances
- **Physicalism**: Mental states as physical brain states
- **Functionalism**: Mental states defined by functional roles
- **Consciousness**: Nature of subjective experience
- **Intentionality**: Mental states directed toward objects
- **Qualia**: Subjective qualities of conscious experience

#### Philosophy of Science (`philosophy_of_science/`)
- **Scientific Method**: Logic and procedures of scientific inquiry
- **Theory and Observation**: Relationship between theories and evidence
- **Explanation and Prediction**: Goals and methods of scientific understanding
- **Laws and Theories**: Nature of scientific knowledge
- **Reductionism vs Holism**: Part-whole relationships in science
- **Paradigm Shifts**: Revolutionary changes in scientific understanding

### Epistemology
The study of knowledge, belief, and justification.

#### Theory of Knowledge (`epistemology/`)
- **Knowledge**: Justified true belief and its conditions
- **Belief**: Nature and formation of beliefs
- **Truth**: Correspondence, coherence, and pragmatic theories
- **Justification**: Internalist vs externalist approaches
- **Skepticism**: Limits and possibility of knowledge
- **Certainty**: Degrees of epistemic confidence

#### Rationality (`rationality/`)
- **Reasoning**: Deductive, inductive, and abductive inference
- **Decision Theory**: Rational choice under uncertainty
- **Bayesian Epistemology**: Probability and belief updating
- **Cognitive Biases**: Systematic deviations from rationality
- **Bounded Rationality**: Rationality within cognitive limits
- **Instrumental vs Epistemic Rationality**: Means vs truth-oriented reasoning

#### Scientific Knowledge (`scientific_epistemology/`)
- **Empiricism**: Knowledge from sensory experience
- **Rationalism**: Knowledge from reason and logic
- **Falsification**: Popper's criterion for scientific theories
- **Confirmation**: Evidence supporting scientific claims
- **Underdetermination**: Multiple theories explaining same evidence
- **Inference to Best Explanation**: Abductive reasoning in science

### Ethics and Values
The study of moral principles and human values.

#### Moral Philosophy (`ethics/`)
- **Metaethics**: Nature of moral judgments and properties
- **Normative Ethics**: Standards for right and wrong action
- **Applied Ethics**: Ethical issues in specific domains
- **Virtue Ethics**: Character-based moral theory
- **Deontology**: Duty-based moral theory
- **Consequentialism**: Outcome-based moral theory

#### Philosophy of Action (`philosophy_of_action/`)
- **Free Will**: Determinism vs indeterminism debate
- **Intentional Action**: Purposeful vs unintentional behavior
- **Practical Reason**: Reasoning about what to do
- **Motivation**: Reasons for action and desire
- **Responsibility**: Moral and causal accountability
- **Autonomy**: Self-determination and control

#### Value Theory (`axiology/`)
- **Intrinsic vs Instrumental Value**: Inherent vs derived worth
- **Well-being**: Theories of human flourishing
- **Justice**: Fair distribution of benefits and burdens
- **Rights**: Moral entitlements and obligations
- **Virtues**: Excellence of character and intellect
- **Meaning**: Purpose and significance in life

## ðŸ”„ Philosophical-Cognitive Interfaces

### Philosophy of Cognitive Science
Foundational questions about cognition and mental representation.

#### Mental Representation (`mental_representation/`)
- **Representation**: How mind represents the world
- **Computation**: Mind as computational system
- **Connectionism**: Neural network models of cognition
- **Embodied Cognition**: Mind as embodied in physical systems
- **Situated Cognition**: Cognition distributed across brain, body, world
- **Extended Mind**: Cognitive processes extending beyond brain

#### Consciousness Studies (`consciousness_philosophy/`)
- **Phenomenal Consciousness**: Subjective experience
- **Access Consciousness**: Information availability for action
- **Self-Consciousness**: Awareness of one's own mental states
- **Qualia**: Ineffable qualities of conscious experience
- **Hard Problem**: Why physical processes give rise to experience
- **Integrated Information Theory**: Consciousness as integrated information

#### Language and Thought (`philosophy_of_language/`)
- **Meaning**: Reference, sense, and use theories
- **Truth**: Correspondence, coherence, deflationary theories
- **Reference**: How words connect to the world
- **Speech Acts**: Language as action
- **Mental Language**: Thought as internal language
- **Semantic Externalism**: Meaning determined by external factors

### Active Inference Philosophy
Philosophical foundations and implications of active inference.

#### Free Energy Principle (`free_energy_philosophy/`)
- **Metaphysical Implications**: Reality as inference
- **Epistemological Foundations**: Knowledge as model minimization
- **Phenomenological Accounts**: Experience as prediction error
- **Causal Structure**: Markov blankets as metaphysical boundaries
- **Self-Organization**: Life as self-evidencing process
- **Consciousness**: Self-model evidence maximization

#### Bayesian Epistemology (`bayesian_epistemology/`)
- **Probabilistic Knowledge**: Degrees of belief as probabilities
- **Belief Updating**: Bayes' theorem as rational learning
- **Prior Knowledge**: Justification for initial beliefs
- **Evidence Integration**: How new information modifies beliefs
- **Rationality**: Coherence of belief systems
- **Uncertainty**: Epistemic vs aleatory uncertainty

#### Pragmatism and Active Inference (`pragmatic_inference/`)
- **Instrumental Truth**: Truth as what works for prediction
- **Action-Oriented Epistemology**: Knowledge for effective action
- **Situated Reasoning**: Context-dependent inference
- **Problem-Solving**: Philosophy as tool for living
- **Experience**: Primary source of knowledge and meaning
- **Growth**: Continuous adaptation and learning

## ðŸŽ¯ Philosophy of Science for Cognitive Modeling

### Scientific Realism vs Anti-Realism
- **Realism**: Scientific theories describe real entities
- **Instrumentalism**: Theories as useful tools, not literal truths
- **Constructivism**: Scientific knowledge as human construction
- **Entity Realism**: Credence in specific entities, not theories
- **Structural Realism**: Scientific knowledge of structure, not content

### Explanation and Understanding
- **Deductive-Nomological**: Explanation as logical deduction from laws
- **Statistical Explanation**: Probabilistic causal relationships
- **Mechanistic Explanation**: How systems work through component interactions
- **Unification**: Explaining diverse phenomena through single principles
- **Causal Explanation**: Understanding through causal mechanisms
- **Interpretive Understanding**: Meaning and context in explanation

### Theory Change and Progress
- **Kuhnian Paradigms**: Revolutionary theory changes
- **Lakatosian Research Programs**: Progressive vs degenerating programs
- **Feyerabend's Anarchism**: Anything goes in theory choice
- **Scientific Progress**: Accumulation vs replacement of knowledge
- **Incommensurability**: Translation problems between theories
- **Rational Reconstruction**: Logical analysis of theory change
